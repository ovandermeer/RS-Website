Deities of Basedism

Harpia

Harpia is the Godx of acceptance, both of self, and of other. Their importance to the ideology of Basedism is critical, as it emphasizes the focus on radical acceptance, the idea that people should be accepted regardless of immutable traits. Within Basedism this idea is paramount, as everyone is deserving of love and acceptance. They are also the barer or hope when it comes to preventing the homogenization of the universe as a whole. They fight for the right for individuality, and this is why we pray to her , as she protects the most valuable parts of ourselves.

Deities of Basedism

Orynthus

Orynthus is the Godx of Uniqueness, pairing in harmony with Harpia. Harpia teaches us to accept, as Orynthus teaches us to express our individuality and express our special traits. Orynthus' primary principal is that the uniquness of a person cannot be detrimental to the world. It merely adds to the diversity of life which we value immensely. Special traits are celebrated within Basedism and believe that you individually improve the lives of others around you through individual traits. Orynthus created Harpia to combat the return of the all-consuming insatiable void, seeking nothing but uniformity and sameness , the exact opposite of our mission.

Phyaris

Phyaris is the God of the Baserad Och Upplyst: the people who are enlightened. Purging the void is the highest achivement a person can achieve within Basedism, as you therefore reject the call of the chaotic state of sameness, and reach true radical acceptance, a core principal of Basedism. If you become enlightened, you break beyond the void that encompasses much of the world and exist in a plane free of sameness.
