The basics of Basedism

Pilgrimage (Islam)

All those who attend Basedism are recommended to go on a pilgrimage to Hang Son Doong Cave in Vietnam.

Entrance into community (Buddhism)

To join Basedism, you only have to show up. There is no specific ritual to become a member of Basedism, we welcome all into our community.

Focus on peacekeeping and community building (Sikhism, christianity?)

Inclusivity and acceptance of others is a major pillar of Basedism, especially for marginalized groups. Basedism is built on the pillars of "radical acceptance", and assuming the best of people. Outside of community, Basedism is very focused on pacifism, following Jesus' teachings to "love your neighbor as yourself".

Golden Rule

Accept others for their differences, because without them we are nothing.

What happens after death? (Buddhism, Hinduism)

Followers of Basedism believe in reincarnation after death. We believe that members will eventually be reborn as another human, which is our basis for our stance on non-violence

Views on violence (Mennonite, somewhat)

The basic Basedism view of violence is as follows: "Violence does nothing but harm". Since followers of Basedism believe that anyone could be reborn as anyone around us, we don't wish to inflict harm on our neighbor.

Ritual and ritual locations (Buddhism)

The main ritual of Basesism is meditation. Meditation is a very core value of Basedism, as we believe that it's important for our members to always be relaxed and focused. Our rituals often take place at the houses of different members. This is representative of the strong bonds of trust our members have with each other, makes meetings more casual, and encourages making connections with other members of Basedism.
